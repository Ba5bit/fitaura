## 3. Section order & rationale

1. **Nav** — brand + jump links + persistent CTA. Lightweight, glassy, gets out of the way.
2. **Hero** — the promise headline + the three cards fanned. Proves the outcome before a single word of explanation. Free-pill + privacy line sit directly under the CTA to kill the two biggest objections (cost, photos) above the fold.
3. **Three-output preview** — the core differentiator: Face Card, Outfit Check, Dating Receipt shown as *distinct but related* artifacts, each with a one-line job. Pinned note: "Every scan returns all three."
4. **How it works** — 3 steps (Upload → Scan → Verdict). Removes "what do I actually do" friction. Kept to one line each.
5. **One scan = everything** — reinforces the bundle and sets up credits: 1 credit → 3 shareable cards + 2 in-app breakdowns. Explains value *before* showing a price.
6. **Credits** — first scan free, then 3 packs. Placed after value is established so price never leads. Packs are equal-weight; pricing is small and scannable, never dominant.
7. **Privacy** — the trust gate right before the final ask: source photos are **not permanently stored on our servers**; results live on the user's device.
8. **Examples** — three sample receipts (Green / Normie / Red). Social proof of the payoff; "one of them is you" invites the self-test.
9. **Final CTA** — single, loud, repeats the free offer.
10. **Footer** — minimal links + the entertainment-only disclaimer (legal + tone safety).
11. **Mobile sticky bar** — keeps the free CTA one thumb-tap away on the long scroll.

Logic: **prove the outcome → explain the bundle → justify the (small) price → remove the privacy fear → show proof → ask.** Cost is introduced only after value; privacy answered immediately before commitment.

## 6. Above-the-fold acceptance criteria

On a 1280×800 desktop viewport and a 390×844 mobile viewport, **before any scroll**:

1. Brand wordmark + nav CTA visible.
2. The exact promise headline is the largest element and fully legible.
3. Subheadline present and readable (≥16px).
4. A primary CTA ("Scan me — it's free") is visible and visually dominant.
5. The "First verdict free" pill is visible — cost objection answered without a price.
6. The privacy line ("Photos never stored on our servers") is visible — trust objection answered.
7. At least one real result card (Face / Outfit / Receipt) is partially visible, proving the output is cards, not a tool UI.
8. No pricing table, no feature list, no AI/tech jargon above the fold.
9. Mobile: sticky bottom bar with the free CTA is pinned and tappable (≥44px target).
